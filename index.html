Land And Water Forms

LESSON PLAN FOR

Preschoolers (Ages 3-6)

APPROACH

This Montessori-based lesson combines classic visual aids, a simple model, and collaborative group work to help students visualize and understand different landforms and bodies of water in an engaging way.

Submitted as an assignment for the completion of the Diploma in the Montessori Method of English Language Teaching.

LESSON AIMS
  • Identify different landforms and bodies of water
  • Develop a model to represent the shapes and kinds of land and bodies of water in an area

STEM in IELTS Writing Task 1

LESSON PLAN FOR

Adults (CEFR B2)

APPROACH

This lesson plan prepares students for IELTS Writing Task 1 by focusing on the analysis and description of graphical data. Students will interpret nutrition facts labels, create corresponding graphs, and then practice writing concise reports summarizing the key features and trends in their graphs.

LESSON AIMS
  • Identify and extract key data points and trends
  • Recognize and understand the main features and categories of information presented

Creative Coding in Scratch

LESSON PLAN FOR

Grade 5-6 (Ages 10-12)

APPROACH

This lesson adopts a guided exploration and problem-based learning approach. Students are introduced to the concept of drawing symmetrical patterns through a brief demonstration or review of the Code.org Frozen activity. They are then challenged to replicate and extend these ideas in Scratch, focusing on the Pen extension. The teacher provides initial scaffolding (e.g., demonstrating basic pen commands and a simple square loop) and then encourages students to experiment with different angles and repeat counts to discover how various patterns emerge.

LESSON AIMS
  • Understand and apply concepts of repetition, angles, and loops
  • Draw various snowflake and pattern designs, fostering creativity through programming

44th SSEAYP - The Ship for Southeast Asian and Japanese Youth Program

October - December 2017

The Ship for Southeast Asian and Japanese Youth Program (東南アジア青年の船) is an annual youth exchange program organised by the Cabinet Office of Japan and governments of Southeast Asian countries for the purpose of promoting friendship and mutual understanding among the youths. Chen was the country representative for PY Seminars and Discussion Programs, and the technical lead for MyPY.

Program Outline

The program started in Tokyo, with an inauguration ceremony and a welcome reception. Chen was in the homestay program for Nagasaki, Phnom Penh, Bangkok, Jakarta and Kuala Lumpur, where the Discussion Group learned about various projects, such as YouTube Space Tokyo and DW Akademie in Cambodia. He also wrote the playscript Sagawa, which was performed as the main act of the national presentation, in front of international audience. You can watch the full performance here on YouTube.
